Tex-Mex Pork Chops

Summary

Delicious quick one-pan dish featuring seasoned pork chops, rice, veggies, and Tex-Mex flavors.

Total time: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 boneless pork chops, about 3/4-inch thick
  • 2 teaspoons taco seasoning spice blend
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup coarsely chopped green bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 (10-ounce) cans diced tomatoes and green chilies (such as Ro-tel®), with juice
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 1/4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups frozen corn kernels, thawed
  • 2 cups sliced zucchini
  • 1 cup uncooked rice
  • flat-leaf parsley or cilantro (optional)
  • lime slices (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium heat.
  2. Season pork chops with taco seasoning, cumin, smoked paprika, and salt.
  3. Cook chops until browned (2 mins per side); set aside.
  4. Sauté onion and bell pepper until slightly colored, add garlic.
  5. Stir in diced tomatoes, chili powder, and black pepper into chicken broth.
  6. Add corn, zucchini, and rice to the skillet; bring to a boil.
  7. Nestle pork chops, cover, simmer for 20 mins.
  8. Uncover, simmer until rice is tender (5 mins).
  9. Garnish with parsley or cilantro, lime slices. Serve warm.
